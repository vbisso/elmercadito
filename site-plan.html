Purpose

The purpose of the "El Mercadito" web page is to sell high-quality Peruvian food ingredients that are not easily found in regular markets. As well as to showcase a variety of recipes that can be made of the products on the web page.

Audience

The main audience is mainly Peruvian immigrants who love their natal food and food-passion people who enjoy trying foreign food.

Product information

  • Yellow Pepper – Aji Amarillo

    • Aji amarillo is a fundamental ingredient in Peruvian gastronomy, since you can prepare classic recipes such as “ají de gallina”, “huancaína cream”, “causa de pulpa de cangrejo”, “cau-cau” and much more. In addition, its consumption helps in burning calories and abdominal fat. Contains capsaicin that improves blood circulation and reduces cholesterol levels. It is recommended to keep this grocery refrigerated for its good conservation.

  • Red Pepper – Rocoto

    • Ideal to accompany everything from soups to main courses to give it that extra spicy taste that you want so much. Its consistency is thicker, since it is an ideal dip for tequeños, appetizers, nachos and more. Contains capsaicin, which helps improve blood circulation in the body. In addition, it stimulates the nervous system to produce endorphins that cause a feeling of satisfaction and relaxation.

    Recipes:
    "Lomo Saltado"
      Ingredients:

    • Papa Amarilla (Yellow potatoes) – 5 units
    • Salt and Pepper
    • Vegetable Oil – 6 tablespoons
    • Fine minced Garlic – 1 tablespoon
    • Rice – 2 cups/li>
    • Choclo (White corn) – 1 unit
    • Butter – 1 tablespoon
    • Thin Loin – 250 gr
    • Red onion – 1 unit
    • Aji Amarillo (Yellow Pepper) – 1 unit
    • Soy Sauce
    • Oyster Sauce
    • White vinegar – 2 tablespoons
    • Tomatoes – 1 unit
    • Green Onions
    • Cilantro
      Preparation:

    1. In a pot, add 5 quartered yellow potatoes (“papas amarillas”), add 1/2 cup of water, and 1/2 teaspoon of salt. Cover and cook for 6 minutes.
    2. In a pot add 3 tablespoons of oil, and 1 tablespoon of finely chopped garlic (fry for 3 minutes).
    3. Add 2 cups of washed rice to the pot, mix, add 2 quarters of a cup, a shelled corn (“choclo”), salt to taste, cover and cook for 20 minutes. If you like you can add a tablespoon of butter. Reserve and keep warm.
    4. On a chopping board, cut 250 grams of thin loin (“lomo”), cut into strips, and then into cubes, add salt and pepper to taste.
    5. In a preheated pan for 3 minutes over high heat add 3 tablespoons of oil and add cubes of fine loin (in small portions). Seal over high heat for 1 minute. Push and pull the pan until it starts to flame. Reserve sautéed loin (“lomo saltado”).
    6. In a pan add 1 red onion in wedges, and 1 yellow pepper (“aji amarillo”) cut into strips, seal over high heat for 1 minute, and sauté. Reserve sautéed vegetables together with one tomato cut into wedges.
    7. In a container add 4 tablespoons of clear soy sauce, 3 tablespoons of oyster sauce, 2 tablespoons of white or red vinegar, 1/4 cup of water. Integrate and reserve.
    8. Add the “lomo saltado” sauce to the pan, reduce to the point of sauce, and then add the “lomo saltado” and integrate.
    9. Add vegetables, and green onion in sticks to taste, sauté, and add finely chopped cilantro to taste. Sauté and serve hot.
    10. Fry precooked potatoes in abundant oil, until golden brown. Serve together with grained rice and “lomo saltado”.
